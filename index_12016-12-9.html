No Wake Zones

Peaceful epicenters beyond center camp.

Impetus

There are many beautiful art pieces, camps, and moments which are made harder to immerse oneself in by nearby amplified sounds. Many artists and burners would like to have a safe space away from these loud sounds, while still having art and an open community, and without infringing on anyones right to make noise.

Radical Inclusion

"To tell either the loud to be quiet or the not so loud to just deal with it is, unfortunately, going to leave someone feeling radically unincluded."

Radical Inclusion is a tricky concept when related to entities which can dominate over one another. In this case, a large sound system and many surrounding art pieces, camps, or connections. The sense of feeling respected, welcome, and at home is lost when one group feels opressed by the wants of another. To tell either the loud to be quiet or the not so loud to just deal with it is, unfortunately, going to leave someone feeling radically unincluded. For any true solution, we should neither rule out calm beauty nor chaotic expression, even though they are so frequently difficult to combine.

Chattish and Whispish

In order to talk about this subject, a few words need to be defined:

  • Chattish - the level of sound when a bunch of people are talking. Quieter than constant yelling or amplified music, louder than Whispish.
  • Whispish - the level of sound in a Library, or the Temple

Existing Cohabitation Styles

There are a few basic known ways of somewhat combining uncombinable things:

  1. Alternating: The two are given turns. Chattish/loud hours are set. Sound curfews at Burning Man are simply a bad idea.
  2. Parallelism: The two things are seperated by personal perspective. Requires people to hear the quiet through the loud and vice versa. Current solution.
  3. Separation: The two are separated. Chattish/loud spaces are defined. To some degree, this has already happened with the further out streets, but it's still a crapshoot whether or not a you'll end up next to a sound camp.


Disclaimer

Following is a potential solution, but it is not what lies at the core of this proposal. The core of this is that we, as a community, need to work together and keep as many people as possible feeling included. Beyond that core issue is the fact that many people who prefer a little peace are starting to feel displaced. If you do not like the proposed solution, please give your reasons and perhaps a new solution. Community is built by discussion, and this proposal is just a topic piece.


The Idea

"[a place for those] who come to Burning Man looking for quiet beauties and calm, creative community."

Whilst walking down L street on 3:00 side, taking in the amazing scenery, the wind, the peace of many small camps setting up for dinner, still an hour till sunset, the thought arose: if only the art could be here too. And why not? Why not have a small, open, and relatively calm space for artists who choose it to place their art? They could become epicenters for the relaxed and communal, the tea and yoga camps, acoustic performances, Zendo psychedelic harm reduction, nap areas, ball pits, blanket forts, Medics, Rangers, retired Burners, and everything and everyone else who comes to Burning Man looking for quiet beauties and calm, creative community.

The Working Name

The current working name for these spaces is "No Wake Zones." Something had to be chosen for the domain name, who knows what it will be in the end. The basic ideology behind these is that they are places where people feel free to talk and occasionally yell, but it would inconsiderate to blast your music way louder than it needs to be for hours on end. In order to get camp placement there are a few extra rules, such as pointing your speakers inward and keeping them below a certain level, especially during the most disrespectful hours. There should be some sort of signage which lets art cars know they are entering a No Wake Zone, and a prior knowledge of what that means. And most of all, there should be enough space to house all the art of the many artists who would prefer to have their pieces in an area that is more community centered and less loud and destructive.

No Wake Zone Placement

The most complex part of this idea is correct placement. After much consideration, the open spaces where the arrows point were the best placements and sizing we could think of:

(click to zoom)
centered at 4:00 and 8:00 on India street with a diameter of three blocks, the spaces are both effective and mathematically simple.
Why we like this placement and sizing:
  • It interferes with the least amount of theme camps on the map.
  • Is is midway between the center camp and sound camp areas.
  • It incentives city wide suburban exploration.
  • It is large enough to comfortably fit multiple art pieces and create a sense of open space.
  • It uses the number three which is the only other prime factor of twelve (2 * 2 * 3).
  • By splitting the circle into thirds starting at the temple, it creates an underlying pattern that relates it to the temple and the man in a similar way as center camp is related (halves/thirds).
  • It is very far from the Esplanade, 2:00 and 10:00, and A/B street high energy zones.
  • It is two blocks away from the outer ring, making it easy to avoid for incoming cars.
  • It will be easier to MOOP than open playa.
  • It opens up great placement for newer camps without having to displace any older ones
  • It neither interferes with nor requires the creation of roads. People can pass through in the same way as open playa.

We Might Be Wrong

The Temple Thirds Placement was the best solution we could think of, but we also realize it is unlikely to be the final one. And not just the placement, but the ideals, potential rule set, and name are all just our contributions to what hopefully becomes a community wide discussion and creation. As said in the disclaimer, all that really matters is an answer to the impetus, that no group of people should be made to feel like their wants are not important.
